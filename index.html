About

Background

I'm pursuing my medical degree (MD) at Umeå University, expected to graduate in 2029. My interests lie at the intersection of neuroscience and clinical medicine — understanding how the brain changes, and what that means for patients.

Research

My research focuses on early markers of Alzheimer's disease. I study memory-related brain activity using functional MRI (fMRI) and longitudinal data from the Betula study, one of the longest-running studies on memory and aging.

Teaching

I work as a teaching assistant at Umeå University, instructing junior medical students in histology and practical microscopy — helping them connect microscopic anatomy to human health.

Location

Based in Umeå, Sweden, as part of Umeå University's medical programme.
